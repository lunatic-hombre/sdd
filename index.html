THE THREE PILLARS OF SHAME

  1. Hatred of Old Code
  2. Hatred of New Code
  3. Hatred of Code to Be

The core tenant of Shame Driven Development (SDD) is that all code is unworthy of its own existence. When you look at what you’ve written, you should be overwhelmed with a desire for self-flagellation. You should ask yourself “What would Mother think of this?,” and the correct answer must be “I should have had an abortion.”

You will have reached peak shame when, upon looking at your code, you kill yourself to prevent you from doing anymore harm to this world.

CONTINUOUS FAILURE

This is the iron ribbon that binds the 3 pillars together in a moebius strip of anti-success. It is very important that no stretch of success lasts too long, or you might stop hating yourself, which is the first step on the path to ruin.

Followers of the Old Way have often treated the word "continuous" as a synonym for "pretty often", but we chose to abide by the true definition - implying that not a single iota of time can be considered anything less than failure.

Chapter 2: Positively Negative Semantics

As the world of misers carries on, our language fades deeper into a dream of artificial niceties. "No more!" cries the SDD acolyte - for there is no greater evil than to deny oneself of the fruits of pain! Any measure of what you call "success" is merely a lapse in failure. "Skill" is just the product of a large tally of errors. The distinguishing of "good" from "bad" is a coward's folly, and must be remitted from the work of the shame-driven developer.

Before providing feedback on someone's efforts, please consult the figure below to ensure you are using language that is appropriate for a shame-driven developer.

You've performed adequately since our last performance review. Kill yourself.

RECOGNITION OF FAILURE

At the end of each unit of incompetent development (e.g. a sprint) an award show for recognizing team member's failings should be held so that their incompetence will live on forever. This should be highly personalized and insulting so that the bearer never forgets (and thus never repeats) their mistakes. We call this SDD ritual, the Failure Parade.

Note: This is not to be confused with the dreadful Retrospective of the Old Way, where failure is eschewed for a false sense of hope. Only through the acceptance of failure can we begin the healing process.
